Discover how climate change is threatening these vital ecosystems

6 Critical Facts About Coral Reefs

1. Ocean Acidification

Oceans have absorbed 30% of CO2 emissions, causing pH levels to drop and making it harder for corals to build their skeletons.

2. Temperature Rise

Just a 2Â°C increase in water temperature can trigger coral bleaching, turning vibrant reefs into ghostly white structures.

3. Biodiversity Hotspots

Coral reefs host 25% of marine life despite covering less than 1% of the ocean floor.

4. Economic Impact

Coral reefs provide $375 billion annually in goods and services, supporting 500 million people worldwide.

5. Recovery Time

Severely bleached reefs can take 10-15 years to recover, if they recover at all.

6. Extinction Risk

Scientists predict 90% of coral reefs could disappear by 2050 if current trends continue.

Current Impact

The Great Barrier Reef has lost 50% of its coral cover since 1985, primarily due to climate change.

What Can We Do?

  • Support marine protected areas
